A Brief Timeline of Jesse Owen's Life

  • 1913 - Born on September 12 as James Cleveland "Jesse" Owens.
  • 1919 - Owens works in the fields with his brothers and sisters while attending school at the age of 6.
  • 1935 - He won 4 Individual NCAA championships: 100 yard dash, long jump, 220 year sprint, and 220 yard hurdle
  • 1936 - Owens won the same 4 medals the following year. He later was invited to take part in the Olympics by NAACP secretary Walter White. He originally declined due as response to ongoing racism, but eventually attended Berlin Summer Olympics and won 4 gold medals. He was also named AP athlete of the Year.
  • 1942 - Owens is appointed the Director of National Fitness by U.S. Office of Civilian Defense. He worked at Ford Motor Company as Assistant Personnel Director, and became Director until 1946.
  • 1950 - Associated press names Owens the greatest track athlete of the past Half-Century
  • 1970 - Inducted into the Alabama Sports Hall of Fame.
  • 1973 - U.S. Olympic Committee appoints Owens to its board of directors.
  • 1976 - He was awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by President Gerald Ford. That same year, he was made part of the Olympic order for his fight against racism at the 1936 Olympics in Berlin.
  • 1980 - Passed Away at the age of 76 on March 31.
